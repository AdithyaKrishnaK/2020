2020 - Year @ Home

This year COVID-19 took the center stage. So there is a high chance that you may have missed some things. Don't worry we have compiled a list.

Basketball Legend dies in a Helo Crash

One of the greatest basketball players of all time, Kobe Bryant died in a helicopter crash in Calabasas, California. He was 41. His 13-year-old daughter, Gianna was also on board. He played for the Los Angeles Lakers. During his time, the Lakers won five NBA Championships. He was also a part of the American Gold medal winning team in 2008 and 2012. Bryant retired in 2016.

Parasite wins 4

At the 92 Academy Awards, Parasite won 4 Oscars including the Best Picture(becoming the first non-English film to do so). The Best Actor went to Joaquin Phoenix who played the lead role in Joker. Renee Zellweger got the Best Actress. Sam Mendes's 1917 won three Oscars. Toy Story 4 won the Best Animated Feature Film.

NASA Launches Perseverance

NASA launched the MARS 2020 Rover named Perseverance on July 30th. The Rover was launched abroad the Atlas V rocket and is expected to reach Mars by February 2021 This is the first time the NASA will attempt to bring samples from Mars back to earth. The rover also has a Mars helicopter called Ingenuity.

Black Panther is dead

Chadwick Boseman better known for his casting of Black Panther in the Marvel Cinematic Universe died on August due to colon cancer. He was 43. He played the part of Jackie Robinson in the biographical film 42, James Brown in Get on Up and Justice Thurgood Marshall in Marshall. His last film Ma Rainey's Black Bottom was released posthumously. Black Panther 2 was planned due to the success of the first film, Boseman's death leaves great uncertainty about it.

Mumbai Indians wins IPL

Mumbai Indians won their 4th IPL tournament defeating Delhi Capitals. In 2020, IPL which is usually held in April-May was postponed due to COVID-19. It was held in Dubai under strict protocols to ensure the safety of players. It is fair to say that Mumbai Indians dominated the tournament with just five loses in sixteen games.

It was the much anticipated election in 2020. The standoff between Donald Trump and Joe Biden. The election results are out making Joe Biden the 46th president of the United States of America. His running partner, Kamala Harris is going to be the first woman vice-president. Though the election is over and the results are declared, the looming question is will Trump go quietly?
